Grilled Chicken Salad

Grilled Chicken Salad

Nutritional content

Grilled Chicken Salad

  • 250g boneless, skinless chicken breasts: ~ 220 calories
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil: ~ 120 calories
  • Spices: Negligible

Mixed Salad Greens

  • 1 cup mixed salad greens: ~ 10 calories

Vegetables

  • 1 cucumber: ~ 45 calories
  • 1 tomato: ~ 22 calories
  • 1 red onion: ~ 46 calories
  • 1/2 cup bell peppers: ~ 19 calories

Yogurt Dressing

  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt: ~ 70 calories
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil: ~ 120 calories
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice: ~ 4 calories
  • 1 teaspoon honey: ~ 20 calories

Total Calorie Estimate (Approximate): ~ 676 calories

Ingredients

  • 250g boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 cup mixed salad greens (lettuce, spinach, arugula, etc.)
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup bell peppers, sliced (mixed colors)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving

For the Yogurt Dressing

  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Marinate the Chicken:

  1. In a bowl, mix olive oil, ginger-garlic paste, salt, black pepper, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and garam masala.
  2. Coat the chicken breasts with the marinade and let it sit for at least 30 minutes.

Grill the Chicken:

  1. Preheat the grill or grill pan.
  2. Grill the marinated chicken breasts for about 6-8 minutes on each side or until fully cooked.
  3. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing it.

Prepare the Salad:

  • In a large salad bowl, combine the mixed greens, cucumber, tomato, red onion, and bell peppers.

Make the Yogurt Dressing:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together Greek yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, honey, salt, and pepper.

Assemble the Salad:

  1. Arrange the sliced grilled chicken on top of the salad.
  2. Drizzle the yogurt dressing over the salad.
  3. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Serve:

  • Serve the grilled chicken salad with lemon wedges on the side.

This grilled chicken salad is not only delicious but also packed with protein and a variety of fresh vegetables. Feel free to customize the salad with your favorite veggies or add a sprinkle of chaat masala for an extra burst of flavor!

Paneer Tikka

Paneer Tikka

Nutritional content

  • 250g Paneer: 650 calories
  • 1 cup Yogurt: 150 calories
  • Spices and Oil: Approximately 200 calories

Total: Around 1000 calories (This is a rough estimate; actual values may vary based on specific brands and quantities used.)

Ingredients

  • 250g paneer (cubed)
  • 1 cup thick yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon besan (gram flour)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, besan, olive oil, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt to form a marinade.
  2. Add paneer cubes to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Allow it to marinate for at least 30 minutes to let the flavors infuse.
  3. Preheat the oven to 200°C (392°F).
  4. Thread the marinated paneer cubes onto skewers. Place the skewers on a baking tray lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until the paneer is cooked and has a golden-brown color. You can also use a grill for this step.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  7. Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.

Enjoy your healthy paneer tikka!

Chickpea and Vegetable Curry

Chickpea and Vegetable Curry

Nutritional content

  • 1 cup Chickpeas (canned): 240 calories
  • 1 cup Mixed Vegetables (e.g., carrots, bell peppers, peas): 50 calories
  • 1 can (400ml) Coconut Milk: 800 calories
  • Spices and Oil: Approximately 150 calories

Total Calorie Intake (Approximate) - 1240 calories

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chickpeas (canned, drained and rinsed)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., carrots, bell peppers, peas)
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until onions are translucent.
  2. Add curry powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and turmeric powder. Stir well to combine.
  3. Add mixed vegetables and sauté for a few minutes until slightly softened.
  4. Add chickpeas and coconut milk. Season with salt and pepper. Bring to a simmer and let it cook for about 15-20 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro.
  6. Serve hot with rice or naan.

Enjoy your flavorful Chickpea and Vegetable Curry!
